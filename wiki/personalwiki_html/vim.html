Searching, regex

Multi-line searching

To match over multiple lines, you can use the special \_ style operators.

\_s
a whitespace (space or tab) or newline character
\_^
the beginning of a line (zero width)
\_$
the end of a line (zero width)
\_.
any character including a newline

multis

The * multi will greedily match multiple characters. The \{-} multi will find the shortest match.

So, for ababa

  • .*a will match ababa
  • .\{-}a will match aba
